About the Marketing Sector

Every other industry in the economy relies on skilled marketing specialists to bring in customers, sell their products and services, and create a buzz around their brand; without a thriving marketing sector, economic growth would simply stagnate.

Given the many different media through which people consume information in the modern world, there are almost countless ways to approach marketing, and as such jobs are often highly specialized in a particular medium or product. The most effective marketers are highly creative, excellent communicators who are able to quickly identify trends in their target consumers’ behaviour and adapt accordingly.

Marketing continues to be one of the fastest-growing industries, and attracts workers from all kinds of academic and vocational backgrounds. Read on to find out more about popular marketing jobs.

About the Marketing Sector

Social Media Manager

A Social Media Manager coordinates a company’s activities ocross all the socials where they are present. Working closely with designers, product managers and other marketing specialists, it’s their job to ensure that social media is used to create a strong feeling of community and positivity around the brand and its products and services. As well as the day-to-day tasks of content creation, community interaction and taking advantage of trends, Social Media Managers also have to have one eye on the future and plan for upcoming events, product launches, holidays and other important aspects of effective social media marketing strategies.

Brand Manager

Brand Managers work at a strategic level to ensure that the brands they are responsible for continue to grow healthily in the mid-to- long term. Among other tasks, Brand Managers need to be able to interpret, analyze and act on data collected on their target audiences and market segments. Most Brand Managers will have an academic background in marketing, and a deep understanding of consumer behaviour, business strategies, budgeting and other specialist subjects. Given the dynamic nature of the role, some of the key skills needed for a successful brand manager are project management, market analysis and multitasking

Marketing Executive

The core of a Marketing Executive’s daily work is the development of marketing campaigns to drive sales and profits for their company. As part of this process, Marketing Execs are expected to be able to devise strategies that are tailored to specific audiences and that encompass a wide range of marketing strategies including social media campaigns, promotional events, website optimisation and finding brand ambassadors. Marketing Executives can be found in companies of all sizes and, depending on their specific responsibilities, may be expected to specialize in a given area such as website SEO or online content coordination. The best Marketing Executives will be able to adapt quickly to changing market conditions and unforeseen events.
